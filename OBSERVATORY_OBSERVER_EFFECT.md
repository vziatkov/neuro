# The Observer Effect

**The field changes when it is seen**

---

## Ontological turn

So far: trace, resonance, memory, constellation, listening.

One level deeper: **the act of observation affects the system.**

Not only **move → leave trace**, but **observe → change the field.**

---

## How it works

When the user **stops moving** and simply **looks** (no input for several seconds), the field **reacts to attention**. The last curve begins to **stabilize slowly**; around it a **structure** forms: grid, orbit, field. As if space **crystallizes around attention**.

New trace type: **attention trace** — not only motion trace.

If **several people stop moving at once** and look at the same region, a **very stable structure** forms there: geometric node, glowing center, lasting figure.

---

## Philosophical layer

The project starts to show **the influence of observation on the system**. Echoes of: quantum observer effect, second-order cybernetics, observation theory (Luhmann).

It becomes not just a tool but a **model of the interaction between observer and environment**.

Question: **Is presence defined by action or by attention?**

---

## Three modes of presence

| Mode | What | Result |
|------|------|--------|
| **Action** | Movement | Signal |
| **Interaction** | Resonance with others | Pattern |
| **Observation** | Stillness, gaze | Structure |

User can: **move → create signal** · **listen → create harmony** · **observe → stabilize pattern**.

---

## Formula

> Motion creates signal. Resonance creates pattern. **Observation creates structure.**

---

## Strongest scene

8 people in the room. First they move — traces, resonance, constellations, music. Then they **stop**. The space slowly **turns their traces into a stable structure**. Crystallization of observation.

---

## Why philosophers of interfaces care

Most interfaces assume **interaction = action**. Observatory says **interaction = presence** (action, resonance, or observation). It becomes a **spatial model of presence** — almost a meditative interface: movement creates the world, attention stabilizes it.
